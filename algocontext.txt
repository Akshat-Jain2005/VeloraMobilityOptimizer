Algorithmic Approaches to the Heterogeneous VRP with Soft Time Windows (HVRPSTW)
The HVRPSTW combines heterogeneous fleet (vehicles with different capacities or types) and soft time windows (customers may be served outside their window at a penalty). This variant is NP-hard, so a wide range of methods have been proposed: exact mixed-integer models, construction heuristics, metaheuristics, and hybrid or learning-based methods. Below we summarize key approaches from the literature, especially those applied to employee-shuttle or corporate-transportation scenarios.
Exact and Mathematical Programming Methods
Some studies formulate HVRPSTW as a mixed-integer program and solve it exactly (often for small cases). For example, Ismail et al. (2021) model a 3PL delivery case as an HVRPSTW and solve it via branch-and-bound (using LINGO)[1]. Their MILP yields optimal routes, times, costs and loads for each heterogeneous vehicle. Likewise, Peker (2023) formulates an employee shuttle routing problem (including employee walking times to stops) as a goal-programming MILP and solves it to optimality with CPLEX[2]. Both studies demonstrate exact solutions (minimizing total travel plus penalty costs) but are limited to instances small enough for branch-and-bound.
Another exact approach is column-generation (branch-and-price). Mutzel et al. (2022) consider a VRP variant with heterogeneous time windows and solve it by a set-partitioning branch-and-price[3]. Their method uses a specialized labeling algorithm to generate routes, illustrating that even vehicle-specific window constraints can be handled exactly.
Hybrid exact/meta methods (“matheuristics”) have also appeared. For instance, Bideq et al. (2025) propose a two-phase framework for the Employee Shuttle Bus Routing Problem (ESBRP)[4]. First a Memetic Algorithm (genetic algorithm + local search) constructs feasible routes. Then a Set-Partitioning MILP allocates these routes to a heterogeneous fleet optimally. The authors also implement a direct MILP (solved by CPLEX) for comparison[5]. This hybrid approach produced near-optimal solutions much faster than pure exact solving. (Similarly, Gasque & Munari (2022) formulate a rich pickup-and-delivery HVRP with soft windows and solve it by ALNS within a modeling framework[6].)
Construction Heuristics and Tabu Search
Simple heuristics (savings, insertion, sweep, etc.) are often used to build initial routes. In the employee-shuttle context, Purba et al. (2020) address an Employee Transportation Problem as a Split-Delivery VRP with time windows (VRPSDTW). They apply a Tabu Search heuristic to improve routes[7]. Testing two scenarios (different depot layouts), their TS approach reduced fuel cost by about 7–8% compared to the company’s existing schedule[8]. Similarly, Molina et al. (2020) introduce the HVRP with Time Windows and Limited Resources (HVRPTW-LR). Their solution is heuristic: first a “semi-parallel insertion” (a greedy insert) builds routes, then a hybrid Variable-Neighborhood Descent (with Tabu Search and a holding list of unserved customers) refines them[9]. This allows balancing customer coverage and resource limits. These works show that classic constructive and local-search heuristics (enhanced by tabu or VND) can tackle HVRPSTW variants effectively in practice.
Metaheuristic Algorithms (GA, ACO, PSO, VNS, ALNS, etc.)
Metaheuristics are widely used for large or complex instances. Leksakul et al. (2017) solve a large-scale employee-bus routing problem (≈5,000 employees) via a two-stage metaheuristic[10][11]. First they cluster employee locations into bus stops using K-means (Maximin), Fuzzy C-means, and Competitive Learning algorithms. The best clustering (K-means+Maximin) minimized the number of stops and total travel distance[10]. Second, they apply Ant Colony Optimization (ACO) to route the buses through these stops optimally[11]. The result was an “optimal” routing (134 buses covering ~500 stops) for the factory, demonstrating the power of combining data-driven clustering with swarm optimization in an employee transport setting.
Beyond this case, many standard metaheuristics have been adapted to HVRPSTW or related hetero-VRPs. For example, Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) and Genetic Algorithms (GA) have been used to solve heterogeneous VRPs with time windows and backhauls (e.g. Belmecheri et al. 2013). Variable Neighborhood Search (VNS) and its adaptive variants (ALNS) are also popular. Hiermann & Puchinger (2013) develop a hybrid heuristic combining ALNS with embedded local search for a hetero-VRP with time windows[12]. More generally, metaheuristics cited in the literature include: Koç & Bektaş (2012) – a hybrid evolutionary algorithm (GA with problem-specific operators)[12]; Hiermann & Puchinger (2013) – an ALNS for HVRPTW[12]; Ghannadpour & Zarrabi (2012) – NSGA-II (a multi-objective GA) for energy-efficient hetero-VRP; Wang et al. (2014) – ruin-and-recreate + Tabu Search for a multi-type fleet VRPTW; Marinakis et al. (2015) – PSO for VRPTW; Chen et al. (2015) – hybrid PSO+SA for multi-compartment VRPTW; etc.[12]. These metaheuristics typically encode a route set and use operators (swap, relocate, 2-opt, etc.) to minimize total cost + penalties. In all cases, vehicle heterogeneity (capacities, fixed costs) and soft windows (penalty functions) are incorporated into the cost function or constraints of the algorithm.
Hybrid and “Matheuristic” Frameworks
Several recent works combine metaheuristics with exact methods (“matheuristics”). Aside from Bideq’s memetic+SPP approach[4], Gasque & Munari (2022) mentioned above use ALNS together with exact models for a rich pickup-delivery HVRP[6]. Xu et al. (2026) even integrate Reinforcement Learning into a metaheuristic: they train a neural policy (transformer) to guide an Adaptive VNS for a multi-time-window VRP[13]. This RL-AVNS learns which neighborhood search move to apply based on solution state, significantly improving over standard VNS on complex instances. Although not tailored to hetero fleets, it shows a trend toward learning-augmented heuristics. In summary, hybrid approaches may generate candidate routes via metaheuristics and then polish or assign them with exact submodels, or adaptively learn search strategies during optimization.
Machine Learning and Data-Driven Techniques
Machine learning is less common in classic VRP literature, but a few methods have emerged. As noted, unsupervised clustering is used by Leksakul et al. to pre-process stops[10]. More generally, one can use ML to predict travel times or demand uncertainty, or to cluster customers. In the VRP/STW context, contemporary research explores reinforcement learning or neural networks to guide search. The RL-AVNS of Xu et al. (2026)[13] is a prime example (see above). Other work uses neural policies for sequence construction (e.g. pointer networks) or graph embeddings for route scoring, but these are typically for homogenous VRP and are just now reaching VRP variants. In practical systems, companies like MoveInSync claim to use AI/ML for route optimization, though academic details are scarce. Overall, data-driven methods (clustering, learning heuristics) offer promise, but published algorithms specifically for heterogeneous fleets with soft time windows are still few.
Dynamic and Special Approaches
Aydın et al. (2025) introduce a Dynamic Linear Programming (DLP) model for an employee shuttle service[14]. Their DLP considers two vehicle types and time-varying demands at a shipyard, optimizing schedules to minimize fuel, maintenance and other costs. This approach is essentially a time-indexed LP solved in a rolling horizon (exact linear optimization rather than heuristic routes).
Fuzzy logic has been applied as well. Sánchez-Ansola et al. (2023) develop a fuzzy optimization model for an employee shuttle (similar to school bus) with soft time windows and uncertainty[15]. They model uncertain arrival/departure times with fuzzy constraints and solve a fuzzy MILP, showing that fuzzy approaches can improve robustness in employee transport planning.
In summary, exact methods (MIP, B&P) guarantee optimality but scale poorly; heuristics/TS are simple and fast; metaheuristics (GA, ACO, PSO, VNS, ALNS, etc.) offer good quality on large instances; hybrid matheuristics combine both; and emerging ML/AI methods (clustering, RL-augmented search) are on the rise. # Report

## Algorithms for the Heterogeneous Vehicle Routing Problem with Soft Time Windows (HVPRSTW)

### 1. Formal Definition

**HVPRSTW** extends the classical VRPTW by including:

* A fleet of vehicles with **different capacities, costs, speeds, eligibility**
* **Soft time windows**: service outside the preferred interval is allowed but penalized
* Objective typically minimizes:

  * Total travel cost
  * Fleet operating cost
  * Time window violation penalties
  * Delay / dissatisfaction penalties

Mathematically, the objective often looks like:

[
\min \sum c_{ij} x_{ij} + \sum_k f_k y_k + \sum_i \alpha_i L_i + \sum_i \beta_i E_i
]

Where late/early penalties explicitly model employee inconvenience.
